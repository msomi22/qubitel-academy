# Grade 1 English Theme 1: Greetings — Listening and Speaking

> Source: content copied from the shared Grade 1 English curriculum screenshots.

## Theme

**1.0 Greetings**

## Suggested Vocabulary

Good morning, good afternoon, hello, good evening, greet, fine, how are you?

## Strand

**1.1 Listening and Speaking**

## Sub-strand

**1.1.1 Pronunciation and Vocabulary**

**Lessons:** 4 lessons

## Specific Learning Outcomes

By the end of the sub-strand, the learner should be able to:

1. Recognize the letters and their corresponding sounds for effective communication.
2. Pronounce words with the target sounds accurately for effective communication.
3. Use words related to greetings in everyday conversations.
4. Realize the importance of greetings in diverse contexts.

## Suggested Learning Experiences

Learners should:

1. Listen to an oral text with words containing the sounds corresponding to the letters `a-h`.
2. Take turns to pronounce sounds:
   - `a`: `/æ/` as in `cat`, `/ɜː/` as in `bird`, `/ɑː/` as in `car`, `/ə/` as in `about`
   - `b`: `/b/`
   - `c`: `/k/`, `/s/`
   - `d`: `/d/`
   - `e`: `/e/` as in `bed`, `/iː/` as in `peel`
   - `f`: `/f/`
   - `g`: `/g/`
   - `h`: `/h/`
3. Pronounce the sounds as modelled by peers, the teacher, or an audio recording.
4. Match sounds to corresponding letters.
5. Talk about pictures/photos of people greeting one another.
6. Watch videos of people greeting each other.
7. Use non-verbal cues such as eye contact, appropriate gestures, and facial expressions when greeting others.
8. Practise various types of greetings such as shaking hands, patting someone on the back, fist bump, and wave among others as they take turns.

## Inclusion Notes from the Shared Curriculum Page

- Learners with speech difficulties could use alternative and augmentative modes of communication.
- Learners with postural defects or short stature could be appropriately positioned for comfort and clear view.
- Screen resolution and light intensity could be regulated for learners who are sensitive to light.
- Learners with postural defects could use alternative functional parts of the body, perform an alternative corresponding activity, or perform at their level of functional ability.

## Suggested Key Inquiry Questions

1. Why do we greet people?
2. How do we greet people at different times of the day?

## Core Competencies to be Developed

**Learning to learn:** The learner’s ability to recreate learning experiences outside the class is enhanced as they use vocabulary learnt to greet people at different times of the day.

## Values

- **Unity:** Developed as the learner takes turn to greet each other with peers and appreciate the importance of oneness regardless of individual differences.
- **Respect:** Developed as the learner practises patience and humility with each other during turn taking as they greet.
