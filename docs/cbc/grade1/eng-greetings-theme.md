### Theme 1.0: Greetings

**Suggested vocabulary to be signed**

**Strand 1.1: Observing and articulating signs (Listening and speaking) (4 lessons)**

* **Sub strand 1.1.1:** Proper articulation of signs with correct mouth movement and signing vocabulary (Pronunciation and vocabulary).
* **Specific Learning Outcomes:** By the end of the sub strand, the learner should be able to:
* a) recognise the signs related to the theme,
* b) fingerspell letters a-h and their corresponding sounds for effective communication,
* c) pronounce words with the sounds /a:/ /æ/, /3:/, /a:/ /e/ /b:/ /b/ /k/ /s/ /d/ /e/ /i/ /f/ /g/ /h/ for oral fluency (HoH),
* d) recognise the letters and their corresponding fingerspelled letter sounds for effective communication (Deaf),
* e) Use new words related to greetings in everyday conversations (HoH),
* f) use signs of words related to greetings in everyday conversations (Deaf),
* g) appreciate the importance of greetings in diverse contexts.


* **Suggested Learning Experiences:**
* In pairs, learners are guided to watch captioned video clips or observe a signed demonstration of vocabulary related to greetings such as *good morning, good afternoon, hello, good evening, greet, fine, how are you?*
* In pairs, learners are guided to identify, fingerspell and sign vocabulary related to greetings such as *good morning, good afternoon, hello, good evening, greet, fine, how are you?*
* In pairs, learners who are Hard of Hearing are guided to listen to and observe a signed oral text with words containing the sounds corresponding to the letters a-h.
* In pairs, learners who are Deaf are guided to observe a signed oral text with words containing the letter sounds corresponding to the letters a-h.
* In pairs, learners who are Hard of Hearing are guided to take turns to pronounce, articulate signs and fingerspell letter sounds a: /æ/ (cat), /3:/ (bird), /a:/ (car) /ə/ (about) b: /b/c:/ /k/, /s/ d: /d/e/ (bed), /i:/ (peel) f: /f/ g: /g/ h: /h/, as modelled by peers, the teacher or an audio recording (provide basic speech training support such as hand analogy and auditory perception skills).
* In pairs, learners who are Deaf are guided to take turns to articulate signs and fingerspell letter sounds a: /æ/ (cat), /3:/ (bird), /a:/ (car) /ə/ (about) b: /b/c:/ /k/, /s/ d: /d/e/ (bed), /i:/ (peel) f: /f/ g: /g/ h: /h/, as modelled by peers, the teacher or a signed audio visual recording (provide basic speech training support such as hand analogy and auditory perception skills).
* In pairs, learners who are Hard of Hearing are guided to match sounds to corresponding letters.
* In pairs, learners who are Deaf are guided to match fingerspelled letter sounds to corresponding letters.
* Individually, the learner is guided to sign and talk about or describe pictures/photos of people greeting one another.
* In pairs, learners are guided to observe a signed audio-visual recording of people greeting each other.
* In pairs, learners are guided to use non-verbal cues such as eye contact, appropriate gestures and facial expressions when greeting others.
* In pairs, learners are guided to practice various types of greetings such as shaking hands, patting someone on the back, fist bump, waving among others as they take turns.

# Greetings

## What we are learning

In this topic, we learn how to greet people politely at different times and in different situations.

We also learn how to:

* sign greeting words
* fingerspell letters **a-h**
* use greetings in conversations
* sit and prepare well for sign reading
* use the verb **to be**
* prepare for writing using good posture and correct hand movement

---

## Vocabulary

Practise signing and using these greeting words:

* Good morning
* Good afternoon
* Hello
* Good evening
* Greet
* Fine
* How are you?

---

## 1. Listening and Speaking

### Pronunciation and Vocabulary

We learn how to recognise, sign, fingerspell, and use words related to greetings.

### What to learn

By the end of this section, the learner should be able to:

* recognise signs related to greetings
* fingerspell letters **a-h**
* recognise letters and their matching fingerspelled sounds
* use greeting words in everyday conversations
* appreciate the importance of greetings

### For Hard of Hearing learners

Learners practise listening to, pronouncing, and signing words with sounds from letters **a-h**.

Example sounds include:

* **a** as in *cat*, *car*, and *about*
* **b** as in *bed*
* **c** as in /k/ and /s/
* **d** as in *dog*
* **e** as in *bed* and *peel*
* **f** as in *fish*
* **g** as in *go*
* **h** as in *hat*

### For Deaf learners

Learners practise observing, signing, and fingerspelling words with letters **a-h**.

They also match fingerspelled letter sounds to the correct letters.

### Learning activities

Learners can:

* watch captioned videos or signed demonstrations of greetings
* identify, fingerspell, and sign greeting words
* match sounds or fingerspelled letter sounds to letters
* describe pictures of people greeting one another
* observe people greeting each other in signed audio-visual recordings
* use eye contact, gestures, and facial expressions when greeting
* practise greetings such as shaking hands, waving, fist bumping, and patting someone on the back

### Think about it

* Why do we greet people?
* How do we greet people at different times of the day?

---

## 2. Sign Reading

### Pre-reading Letter-sound Knowledge

We learn how to prepare our bodies and materials for sign reading.

### What to learn

By the end of this section, the learner should be able to:

* identify the correct posture for sign reading
* sign vocabulary related to greetings
* sign-read words containing letters **a-h**
* appreciate the importance of correct posture when preparing to sign read

### Learning activities

Learners can:

* choose the correct sitting position from pictures, videos, or demonstrations
* observe signed demonstrations of greeting vocabulary
* identify, fingerspell, and sign greeting words
* position themselves and reading materials correctly
* practise turning pages from right to left for easy sign reading
* track print through finger reading
* pick out and fingerspell words with letters **a-h**
* match target letters with fingerspelled letter sounds
* sign-read short words with two or three letters

### Think about it

* How do we sit while sign reading?
* How do we position our books while sign reading?

---

## 3. Language Use

### Verb “to be”

We learn how to use the verb **to be** when greeting and introducing ourselves and others.

Examples:

* I am fine.
* You are my friend.
* He is happy.
* She is kind.
* We are learners.

### What to learn

By the end of this section, the learner should be able to:

* identify present tense forms of the verb **to be**
* sign present tense forms of the verb **to be**
* use the verb **to be** when greeting and introducing self and others
* appreciate the use of the verb **to be** in daily communication

### Learning activities

Learners can:

* observe pictures, charts, and diagrams showing the verb **to be**
* label pictures using the correct form of the verb **to be**
* observe, fingerspell, and sign words related to the verb **to be**
* take part in language games and songs
* watch signed videos of children greeting and introducing themselves
* introduce themselves politely through signing
* describe themselves and others using the verb **to be**
* practise greetings such as “How are you?” and “I am fine.”
* role-play greeting situations

### Think about it

* How do we introduce ourselves?
* How do we greet people in the morning, afternoon, or evening?

---

## 4. Writing

### Pre-writing

We learn how to prepare for writing by sitting well, holding writing materials correctly, and practising hand movement.

### What to learn

By the end of this section, the learner should be able to:

* recognise the correct sitting posture for writing
* use eye-hand coordination when preparing to write
* understand why correct posture and material positioning are important

### Learning activities

Learners can:

* practise scribbling and shading
* observe pictures or videos showing correct sitting posture
* practise sitting in the right posture
* position the exercise book correctly
* hold pencils or colouring materials correctly
* practise writing from left to right
* join dotted lines to make letters and patterns
* draw and colour shapes and letters
* trace letters and shapes
* mould letters using plasticine or clay

### Think about it

* Why is it important to sit properly when writing?
* How do we place our books when writing?

Exactly — we persist this as **one Notes learning node** with a **book payload** split into internal pages.

The learner path stays:

```txt
gd1 → eng → greetings → notes
```

The strands/sub-strands from the source are **not separate navigation nodes**. They become **pages/sections inside the Notes book**. The uploaded Greetings source has four major curriculum areas under the theme: Listening and Speaking, Sign Reading, Language Use, and Writing. 

## Persistence design

### 1. Navigation nodes

```txt
gd1
└── gd1-eng
    └── gd1-eng-greetings
        ├── gd1-eng-greetings-notes
        ├── gd1-eng-greetings-practice
        ├── gd1-eng-greetings-revision
        └── gd1-eng-greetings-assessment
```

Only these are learner-facing nodes.

### 2. Notes content payload

The node `gd1-eng-greetings-notes` should contain:

```ts
content: greetingsNotesBook
```

Where `greetingsNotesBook` contains internal book pages:

```txt
overview
vocabulary
listening-speaking
sign-reading
language-use
writing
recap
```

These pages are **not learning nodes**. They are content pages inside the Notes node.

## Renderer contract

The book page renderer should only need this:

```ts
node.content.type === 'book'
node.content.pages
```

Then each page renders its `blocks`.

So the route:

```txt
/gd1/eng/greetings/notes
```

loads:

```txt
gd1-eng-greetings-notes
```

and displays:

```txt
Greetings Notes Book
├── Overview
├── Vocabulary
├── Listening and Speaking
├── Sign Reading
├── Language Use
├── Writing
└── Recap
```

## Key rule

Do **not** persist the whole Notes content as one long markdown string.

Persist it as:

```txt
Book
└── Pages
    └── Blocks
```

That gives us clean mobile rendering, page navigation, future read-aloud, progress tracking, accessibility support, and easier editing later.
